Pork Kare KareRECIPE

5 | 559 People

Filipino stews like kare kare include rich, thick peanut sauces. It is a common meal eaten on special occasions in the Philippines.


PREP TIME COOKING TIME TOTAL TIME SERVINGS
12 minutes 1 hour 20 minutes 1 hour 32minutes 6

INGREDIENTS:


  • 4 lbs. pata pork hock
  • 1 piece Knorr Pork Cube
  • 3/4 cup peanut butter
  • 2 bunches baby bok choy or pechay
  • 12 pieces snake beans sitaw, cut into 2 inch pieces
  • 2 pieces eggplant sliced
  • 1/4 cup achiote atsuete seeds
  • 5 cloves garlic crushed
  • 1 piece onion minced
  • 3 1/2 quarts water
  • 3 tablespoons cooking oil
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup bagoong alamang cooked


DIRECTIONS:


STEP 1. Combine 1/2 cup warm water and atsuete. Soak for 10 minutes.Extract the color from the seeds by stirring using a spoon. Filter the seeds out using a kitchen strainer, set the atsuete water aside, we will use it later.

STEP 2. Boil 2 quarts water in a cooking pot. Add pata. Cover and continue to boil in medium heat for 20 minutes.

STEP 3. Remove the boiled pata from the pot. Throw away the water used.

STEP 4. Heat oil in a cooking pot.

STEP 5. Saute garlic and onion once the oil is hot. Cook until onion starts to soften.

STEP 6. Add boiled pork hocks (pata). Saute for 2 minutes.

STEP 7. Pour 1 1/2 quart water. Let boil. Cover the pot and continue to cook between low to medium heat for 60 minutes or until the pata becomes tender. Add more water if needed.

STEP 8. Add Knorr Pork Cube. Stir.

STEP 9. Add peanut butter into the pot and pour atsuete water. Stir. Continue to cook in medium heat for 5 to 8 minutes.

STEP 10. Combine 1/2 cup water with cornstarch. Stir until cornstarch completely dissolves. Pour mixture into the pot. Stir and continue to cook for 5 minutes or until the sauce thickens a bit.

STEP 11. Season with ground black pepper and salt.

STEP 12. Add sitaw and talong. Cover and cook for 5 minutes.

STEP 13. Add bok choy. Cook for 2 minutes.

STEP 14. Tranfer to a serving bowl. Serve with bagoong alamang. Share and enjoy!
